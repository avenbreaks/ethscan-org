Ethereum 2.0 Network Charts

Description: Ethereum chart shows Ethereum statistics, ETH2 validator data and other info pertaining to Ethereum network. Ether chart for professionals and beginners.

The ETH 2.0 blockchain explorer is open to the public, and aside from validators staking, anyone can view wallets, daily transactions, epochs, and proposed blocks on the network. Besides tabular representations, the Ethscan network chart displays details on various staking information in chart form.

This new blockchain relies on ETH2 validators instead of miners who secure it. Blockchain explorer provides the latest Ethereum chart information on transactions that occur on the ETH2 network. Some charts provided by Ethscan include the number of blocks, Ethereum live charts, price history, and network liveliness.

Staked Ether Chart

One of the first charts you will see on the Ethscan is the Staked ETH chart. This Ethereum chart shows the history of daily staked Ether, representing the total amount of Ether on the network. With this chart, you can see the Ether staked in relation to the epoch number.

Blocks

The blocks chart is one of the charts seen on the Ethscan. It primarily shows three details as regards blocks; the proposed, scheduled, and the missed blocks. The proposed blocks show that the validator proposed the block at the appropriate time. The scheduled blocks show that the validators are still submitting relevant data, whereas missed blocks are used to show that the proposer did not propose the block within the required timeframe, and the block was passed to another validator or skipped entirely.

Participation rate

The participation rate shows the percentage of ETH2 validators who are actively validating blocks. This percentage typically falls between 97 and 99 per cent.

Validator Balance

The validator balance chart displays the average daily balance of each Eth2 validator. According to the Ethereum chart analysis, the amount of staked ether from validators has been steadily increasing and currently sits around 33.4 ETH.

Network liveness

The difference between the last completed epoch and the head epoch is referred to as network liveness. The protocol allows epochs to be completed after two epochs. When it goes beyond, there is likely an issue. Since the ETH 2.0 staking began, the finalized epoch has been completed after two epochs just once.

Effective Balance Distribution

How can you know how much ETH is in different nodes? The answer is Effective Balance Distribution.

Over 99.9 per cent of validators staked 32 ETH or more, while 151 validators staked less than the 32 ETH minimum.

Deposits

This ETH chart shows the total number of daily ETH deposits to the ETH2 project. It shows the epoch number by comparing it to the quantity of ETH deposited.
